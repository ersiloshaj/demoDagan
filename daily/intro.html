Welcome to your placement as a Data Analyst

This module is run like a consultancy. You are not here to “learn R” directly. You are here to build decision-support tools that are capable of generating outputs from imperfect data, justify your choices, and communicate findings clearly.
Who are you?

You are a junior analyst intern in a small analytics consultancy that builds dashboards and technical notes for clients. Clients do not ask for a specific set of plots but they do ask for answers, clarity, and recommendations they can act on within the scope of an overarching question they may have. In this module it is up to you to decide which approach best tackles the issues at hand.

You may make educated assumptions based on the results of independent research / asking questions to the "contracted companies", BUT you must not make them silently... it is important that you explain and back-up these choices.

How the work flows

Most real projects follow the same lifecycle as this one. You will gain experience in this loop and by the end of the placement understand how and why these procedures and processes take place

Frame the Question
What decisions are being supported?
Interrogate the Data
What does one row actually represent?
Justify the Transformations
Cleaning is meaning. Explain every change.
Extract Insight from your analysis
Patterns, comparisons, exceptions.
Deliver and Communicate your findings effectively
Make it legible to non-technical people.
What does “good” look like?
  • ... well that's subjective but let's consider a few things;
  • The question your output answers is obvious.
  • Your transformations are documented and defensible.
  • You avoid over-claiming what the data cannot actually prove.
  • A stakeholder is able to consider the inferences you have made and could plausibly act on it.

Your first client! A project in Urban Mobility

Client Dossier:

The Department for Transport needs a consistent view of network usage, reliability, and pricing patterns across different modes and cities. They have the data, but they lack the expertise to manipulate it in such a way that they can understand it. With your help they will be able to gain the clarity needed to support planning and policy.

  • How does ridership change over time?
  • Which modes are most reliable or most variable?
  • How does pricing align with demand?
  • Where do incidents likely affect riders most?
What's the catch?

Nothing comes free, the dataset you are being provided with is not perfect, none of them ever are. Some fields are redundant. Some choices are ambiguous. Sometimes it just looks like a little bit of a mess, you need to appreciate that the data might not come from just one source so it may not be uniform, it may not even be fully patched together yet. In the case of this task, I apologise, it is all intentional, because again that is what “real data” feels like. In the real world you are expected to do additional research and ask questions to the teams that provided the data. This will all be possible to you in this module.

  • Expect missingness and unusual entries
  • Expect trade-offs in cleaning decisions.
  • Expect multiple reasonable answers.

What you will deliver

Week 1

Build core plots and learn to explain what they mean.

  • Time series
  • Bars and proportions
  • Box plots
  • Scatter plots + trends
Week 2

Turn insight into something a stakeholder can explore.

  • Shiny app skeleton
  • Filters (city, mode, dates)
  • Reactive dataset driving all outputs
Choose a client brief and build a polished deliverable.

  • Dashboard + short technical notes
  • Clear assumptions and limitations
  • Decision-support framing
Key idea

The point of this project and these tasks is not "complexity". The point is credible, reusable work that others can trust, navigate with ease and reliably make relevant inferences from.

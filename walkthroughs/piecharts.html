Share of total ridership by mode by pie chart

Goal: compute each mode's share of total ridership, then display it as a pie chart. Pie charts are best when there are only a few categories and you want a quick sense of proportions.

Key idea

  • First sum total riders by transport_mode.
  • Then divide by the grand total to get a share.
  • In ggplot, a pie chart is a bar chart in polar coordinates.
An alternative might be a bar chart labelled with percentages.

Solution

library(tidyverse)
library(scales)

usage <- read_csv("data_clean/transport_usage_clean.csv", show_col_types = FALSE)

mode_share <- usage %>%
  group_by(transport_mode) %>%
  summarise(total_riders = sum(total_monthly_riders), .groups = "drop") %>%
  mutate(share = total_riders / sum(total_riders))

ggplot(mode_share, aes(x = "", y = share, fill = transport_mode)) +
  geom_col(width = 1) +
  coord_polar(theta = "y") +
  scale_y_continuous(labels = percent_format(accuracy = 1)) +
  labs(
    title = "Share of total ridership by mode",
    x = NULL,
    y = NULL
  ) +
  theme_void(base_size = 12)

Subject: Regional Health Inequalities Dashboard for England, 2001 to 2024

The Office for Health Improvement and Disparities is commissioning an internal analytics dashboard to support timely, evidence-based decisions across population health programmes. We need a clear view of regional patterns and how outcomes vary by population group.

context and objective

Our decision makers need a single, reliable tool that summarises health outcomes by region and highlights where inequalities are widening. The dashboard must support exploration by socioeconomic status, gender, religion, ethnic group, deprivation, and free school meals status where available or appropriate.

Analytical standard: This tool is designed for decision support. You should describe associations and patterns. Avoid causal claims unless the data and design explicitly justify them. Document assumptions and known limitations clearly.

data available

Indicators include, but are not limited to:

  • 16 to 17 year olds not in education, employment or training
  • Children under 16 in low income families
  • Life expectancy
  • Infant mortality
  • Obesity prevalence in adults, Year 6 children, and Reception-age children
  • Percentage of 5 year olds with visually obvious dental decay
  • Self-reported wellbeing
  • Smoking prevalence in adults
  • Suicide rate
  • Under 75 mortality from cardiovascular disease
  • Under 75 mortality from cancer

core analytical questions

  • How does life expectancy vary by region, and are there clusters that persist over time?
  • Do childhood indicators align with adult outcomes within the same regions?
  • Does early-life disadvantage align with later health outcomes when stratified by deprivation and socioeconomic status?
  • Which modifiable behaviours show the strongest alignment with premature mortality on a regional basis?
  • Is smoking prevalence more closely aligned with cardiovascular mortality or cancer mortality?
  • Do obesity indicators align with cardiovascular mortality, and does this differ by region or deprivation?

dashboard requirements

Your dashboard should include:

  • Regional overview: Life expectancy by region with clear comparisons and time filtering.
  • Childhood foundations: Views linking low income families, dental decay, and childhood obesity to later outcomes.
  • Modifiable risk factors: Smoking and adult obesity with direct comparison to under 75 mortality.
  • Equity lens: Filters for deprivation and key demographic tags, with clear handling of missingness.
  • Outliers and exceptions: Regions that perform better or worse than expected should be highlighted.
Suggested KPI: Life expectancy gap between top and bottom regions
Suggested KPI: Under 75 cardiovascular mortality spread by region
Suggested KPI: Smoking prevalence spread by region and deprivation
Suggested KPI: Childhood obesity prevalence by region and deprivation

expected deliverables

  • Interactive dashboard suitable for non-technical stakeholders, with sensible defaults and clear labels.
  • Technical note (1 to 2 pages) covering data sources, transformations, definitions, and limitations.
  • Summary slide or memo listing the 3 to 5 most important findings and recommended areas for deeper review.

success criteria

Success will be judged on clarity, interpretability, reproducibility, and the quality of analytical judgement. The best submissions will communicate what the data suggests, what it does not suggest, and what decision makers should do next to validate or act.

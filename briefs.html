Data source: GOV.uk, Office for Health Improvement and Disparities

Notable data includes:

  • All data is catagorised by region, offering high granularity from 2001 to 2024.
  • Data is also tagged by socioeconomic status, gender, religion, ethnic group, deprivation, free school meals status
  • 16-17 year olds not in education, training or employment
  • Children (u16s) in Low Income families
  • Life expectancy
  • Infant mortality
  • Obesity prevalence in adults or at Year 6 and Reception Ages
  • % of 5 year olds with visually obvious dental decay
  • Self-reported well-being
  • Smoking prevalence in adults
  • Suicide rate
  • u75 mortalities from all cardiovascular dieases
  • u75 mortalities from Cancer

  • Identify whether early-life disadvantage aligns with later health-outcomes

    • See if obesity trends with cardiovascular mortality

Data source: Kaggle: Player Scores Dataset

A large football dataset scraped from Transfermarkt covering over 30,000 players, historically up to the 2023/24 season. The dataset includes:

  • Player information and career history
  • Match appearances and scores
  • Market valuations over time

If combined with an additional dataset weighting expected fixture difficulty and league/competition strength, this data could be used to normalise player statistics across contexts.

Secondary consideration: Transfer fees in football have increased dramatically over time. Any analysis of performance-to-market-value ratios would need to account for era-specific inflation, particularly when comparing players from the early 2000s to the modern game.

Project ideas:

  • Link position-specific performance metrics to market value
  • Identify potentially undervalued players based on statistical output
  • Produce data-driven scouting-style reports

Subject: Recruitment Decision-Support Dashboard - Premier League Survival & Resale Value

Thank you for joining our Recruitment & Performance Analytics team. As you’ll know, Leicester City has experienced the highest highs - including our historic Premier League title in 2015/16 - but we also understand how quickly the margins can shift in this league.

As a newly promoted side for the 2024/25 season, our immediate objective is clear: retain Premier League status. At the same time, our recruitment strategy must protect the club financially by prioritising players who can hold or increase their value over the next 1–3 seasons.

your role

You will operate as a Junior Recruitment Analyst. Your task is to build a dashboard that supports non-technical decision makers (Sporting Director, Recruitment Lead, Head Coach) with evidence-based shortlisting.

data available

  • Historical player performance and match data (appearances, minutes, goals/assists where available)
  • Player career histories (league/club/season context)
  • Market valuations over time (to approximate market perception)
Important: You may engineer derived metrics (e.g., per-90 rates, consistency measures), and you may normalise performance across leagues/competitions if you document your assumptions clearly. This tool is decision support - avoid claiming causality or certainty.

core objectives

Your dashboard must help answer:

  • Value-for-money: Which players appear undervalued relative to on-pitch contribution?
  • Context awareness: Which players still rate highly after adjusting for league strength / role / minutes?
  • Immediate impact: Who is most likely to improve our 2024/25 performance under realistic minutes?
  • Resale value: Which targets combine contribution now with a favourable age/value trajectory?
  • Risk: Which options look volatile (performance or value) and should be treated cautiously?

constraints

  • Limited budget relative to established Premier League clubs
  • We need to strengthen in all areas of the pitch; with priority to a goal-scoring striker, a midfield anchor and a central defender. As well as other options which may prove worthy signings across the pitch.
  • We need a blend of short-term stabilisers and assets that retain value
  • Comparability across leagues is essential; assumptions must be transparent
  • Outputs must be interpretable for non-technical stakeholders

expected deliverables

  • Interactive dashboard that supports filtering by position, age band, league, season, and valuation range; and surfaces candidate shortlists with rationale.
  • Technical note (1–2 pages) describing data sources, transformations, normalisation choices, limitations, and how the dashboard should be used responsibly.
  • Optional (stretch): 5–10 player shortlist split into: “survival-ready signings” vs “survival + resale assets”.

success criteria

We are not looking for the most complex model. We care about: clear reasoning, sensible metric design, transparent assumptions, and a dashboard that helps us make better decisions under pressure.

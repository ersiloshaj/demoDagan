Subject: Store Sales and Operational Insights Dashboard, Weekly Data (2022 to 2024)

ASDA is commissioning an internal dashboard to support store leadership and regional management with fast, consistent insight into weekly sales performance. The dashboard will be used to monitor overall trends, understand store and department drivers, and explore how external conditions align with sales patterns.

This project uses a historical retail dataset originally sourced from a public dataset, and will be re-skinned to a UK grocery and general merchandise context for training. Treat it as a realistic internal dataset covering 45 stores, weekly performance, and a small set of operational and economic indicators.

Analytical standard: This tool is designed for decision support. You should describe patterns and plausible operational explanations, but you should avoid claiming causality. Document assumptions and limitations clearly, including data quality issues, missingness, and any transformations.

data available

  • Weekly sales by store and department
  • Holiday flag for key trading weeks
  • Temperature data
  • Fuel price data
  • Unemployment rate data
  • Consumer price index data

primary dashboard objectives

  • Provide leadership with a clear view of total sales trends across the period
  • Identify stores and departments driving revenue, growth, and volatility
  • Support regional managers with a store level deep dive tool
  • Explore whether external indicators align with changes in sales and category mix

dashboard modules and questions

module 1: operational overview

  • Views: Weekly total sales, store comparison, department filter, holiday highlighting
  • Questions: Do sales trend in a particular direction? Which stores dominate revenue? Are trends consistent across stores when external indicators change?

module 2: store and department performance

  • Views: Department contribution within each store, top and bottom departments, store ranking by total sales, growth and volatility
  • Questions: Do the same departments perform similarly across locations? Are some stores overly dependent on a small number of departments?

module 3: store deep dive tool

  • Views: Store selector, departmental sales profile, seasonality, holiday response, overlays for temperature, fuel, unemployment and CPI
  • Questions: What characterises this store? Which departments drive volatility? Which external conditions align with changes in sales? Are there seasonal trends that could support better stocking and merchandising choices?
Suggested KPI: Total weekly sales and rolling average
Suggested KPI: Store rank by total sales and growth rate
Suggested KPI: Store volatility, week to week change distribution
Suggested KPI: Department contribution share within a store

expected deliverables

  • Interactive dashboard suitable for non-technical stakeholders, with clear defaults and labels
  • Technical note (1 to 2 pages) describing data sources, derived metrics, and limitations
  • Brief findings summary with 3 to 5 operational insights and recommendations for follow up analysis

success criteria

Success will be judged on clarity, interpretability, reproducibility, and analytical judgement. The best submissions will communicate what the data suggests, what it does not suggest, and how a regional manager could use the dashboard to act, prioritise, and ask better questions.
